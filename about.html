Financial Surveillnce under current AML/CFT regimes has come to a point where it endangers democracy more than it provides safety to citizens. Under the pretext of protecting us against terrorism and money laundering, AML/CFT laws only continue to expand. AML/CFT laws increasingly threaten our basic fundamental rights despite far-reaching evidence that AML/CFT laws are inefficient and even counterproductive to fighting crime with the mass collection of data leading to a rise in identity thefts and the systematic oppression of dissent. From financial censorship and arbitrary bank account closures based on income and nationality to the criminalization of human rights like the right to privacy, violations of the presumption of innocence, the outsourcing of policing to private banks and companies and the lack of legal recourse for accused, AML/CFT laws are an endangerment to the basic pillars of democratic values.


FR3E consists of a group of citizens with very diverse backgrounds: specialists of finance and law, journalists, engineers, financial compliance officers, and human rights advocates. But we have one thing in common: the willingness to fight for Financial Rights Enactment and Empowerment in Europe to protect our human rights in finance. We believe that we must stop the current drifts of total financial surveillance, that inevitably lead to a mass surveillance society where freedom becomes suspicious, and fundamental rights are but a memory.
